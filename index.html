Common Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
When should I see an ENT specialist?
+
You should consult an ENT specialist if you experience persistent ear pain, hearing loss, ringing in the ears, frequent sinus infections, nasal congestion lasting more than 10 days, chronic sore throat, voice changes, difficulty swallowing, or any neck lumps. Early consultation leads to better outcomes.
Can sinusitis be treated without surgery?
+
Yes, in most cases chronic sinusitis responds well to medical management including antibiotics, nasal steroids, saline rinses, and antihistamines. Surgery (FESS) is recommended only when medical treatment fails or there is significant structural blockage. Dr. Neha always attempts conservative treatment first.
My child has frequent ear infections — should I worry?
+
Recurrent ear infections in children (more than 3 per year) deserve evaluation. They can sometimes affect hearing and speech development if left untreated. Dr. Neha specialises in pediatric ENT and can assess whether medical treatment or minor procedures like grommets are required.
What home remedies work for throat infections?
+
Warm salt water gargles, honey with warm water, steam inhalation, and staying hydrated can soothe mild throat discomfort. However, if fever persists, throat pain is severe, swallowing is difficult, or symptoms last beyond 5–7 days, medical evaluation is essential to rule out bacterial infection requiring antibiotics.
Is tonsil surgery always necessary for tonsillitis?
+
No. Tonsillectomy is considered only when there are 7+ infections per year, 5+ per year for two consecutive years, or when tonsils cause breathing problems during sleep. For most patients, antibiotics and supportive care during flare-ups is sufficient management.
